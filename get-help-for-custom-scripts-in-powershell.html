Get-Help for Custom Scripts in Powershell

Recently, I coded some Powershell scripts for common tasks. No sophisticated stuff, just the typical automation you do when repetition starts to hurt you. I'm just a lazy Consolero, by the way.

After having finished my most needed commands within a brand new Posh2-style custom module, I decided to donate my commands support for the built-in Get-Help command. It turned out to be extremely easy. The key success factor here is a nice little feature called Comment-Based-Help (aka. "AutoHelp").

With this feature, you can document your commands inline within your script files. The syntax is easy and it helps you to always have an up-to-date documentation of your custom scripts. You may consult built-in documentation by invoking

PS> get-help about_comment_based_help

Besides, this blog describes the autohelp feature quite well, too.
