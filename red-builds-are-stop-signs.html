Red Builds Are Stop Signs

I want to share my perspective on "red builds" with you. Red builds (of a continuous integration system) are very important. They need to exist to let us know that something is going wrong. In the spirit of Kanban (the process), you can think of a "red build" literally being a "Kanban" (a signal card). It is a visualization of a problem which occurred in the process of building our software.

Hence, I truly believe that we need red builds. If such a red build happens, then I always think that something went severely wrong. Hence, I believe it's good to completely stop continuing to build software (develop) and try to fix the problem (re-green the build). For me, it's crucial to find and fix the problem before it causes more problems.

The morale here is to accept a "red build" as a stop sign. A "red build" on the CI server means: "STOP! Do not check-in anymore. There is a serious problem. Fix the problem, then continue to check-in." I'd really like to see this culture of stop signs being lived and respected, since I truly believe that it helps developing clean and fast solutions on the long run.
